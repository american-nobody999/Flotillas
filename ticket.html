Andrey Ilyich Khrzhanovskiy, known online as Andrey X, is a Russian-Israeli journalist and activist born on May 2, 1998. Coming from a family of renowned Russian filmmakers (grandson of Andrei Khrzhanovsky and son of Ilya Khrzhanovsky), he grew up in Saint Petersburg and studied anthropology at University College London, focusing on political anthropology.

He began his journalism career around 2021, contributing to anti-government Russian outlets like Meduza, Novaya Gazeta, Doxa, and Discours. Following Russia's 2022 invasion of Ukraine, he fled potential persecution and immigrated to Israel, obtaining citizenship. Initially unfamiliar with Israeli politics, his experiences shifted his focus after witnessing settler violence in the West Bank.

Since 2023, Andrey X has gained prominence as an independent journalist and human rights advocate documenting Israeli settler attacks, military actions, and alleged ethnic cleansing in the occupied West Bank and during the Gaza war. He reports on-the-ground via platforms like Instagram (@the.andrey.x, with hundreds of thousands of followers), X (@the_andrey_x), Facebook, and his website, often highlighting Palestinian experiences and criticizing occupation policies.

He co-founded initiatives like Kompas Media (2023–2024) for Russian-language coverage and has faced arrests, threats, and confrontations while filming. His work blends journalism with activism, drawing both support for exposing abuses and criticism for alleged bias or pro-Palestinian sympathies. Despite challenges, Andrey X continues to report on the Israeli-Palestinian conflict, advocating for human rights and raising awareness of Palestinian struggles.
